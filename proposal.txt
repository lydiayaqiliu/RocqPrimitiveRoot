In this project, I plan to formalize primitive roots modulo a prime number in Coq and use them to verify the Welch construction of Costas arrays. I will define coprimality, primitive roots, and modular exponentiation over integers, and prove that if g is a primitive root modulo a prime p, then the function i ↦ g^i mod p satisfies the Costas property on {0, …, p−1}. As a concrete example, I will show that 3 is a primitive root modulo 89 and that it produces a valid Costas array.
The inspiration for this project comes from an existing mathematical attempt to construct the most “patternless” possible music, where pitch and time differences never repeat. Costas arrays provide exactly this kind of structure, and the goal is to rigorously verify the number theory behind the construction.
Key challenges include reasoning about modular arithmetic and exponentiation in Coq, and proving primitive root properties for concrete primes. If the full theorem is too difficult, a fallback goal is to prove bijectivity of primitive root exponentiation or verify small cases using computation. I expect to rely mainly on Coq’s ZArith and Znumtheory libraries, with possible support from Software Foundations or Mathematical Components.
